The Perfect Carbonara

Description

Carbonara is a delicious classic Roman pasta dish. The Pecorino Romano, Guanciale, Egg, and Black Pepper together work beautifully. When in Rome it is a must try. The below recipe is designed for three hungry people.

Ingredients

  • 400g of spaghetti (preferibly spaghettoni). Mezze maniche also works nicely
  • 280g of Guanciale
  • 3 eggs
  • Plenty of grated Pecorino Romano
  • Black pepper
  • A little white/red wine

Directions

  1. Start by removing the rind of the guanciale. Cut the guanciale in stripes.
  2. Place the stripes of guanciale in a pan or wok, moderate flame. Once the fat gets transparent, pour a little wine. When the alcohol evaporates and the guanciale starts to get crispy, move it to a bowl.
  3. Place water in a pot and wait for it to boil.
  4. Beat the eggs in a bowl, add grated Pecorino to the mix. Adding a few spoons of the fat released in the pan to the egg and pecorino mix gives it a silkier texture.
  5. Once the water boils, add the pasta. When the pasta is al dente, save some of its water, then drain it and move it in the pan/wok. With no flame mix the pecorino and egg with the pasta, add a little water from the pasta pot, keep mixing and finally add the guanciale
  6. Once you plate the pasta sprinkle on it a little black pepper and some pecorino.
  7. Now enjoy your perfect Carbonara!
